Thrissur is also known as the Cultural Capital of Kerala because of its cultural, spiritual and religious leanings throughout history. The city centre contains the Kerala Sangeetha Nadaka Academy, Kerala Lalithakala Akademi and Kerala Sahitya Academy. The city hosts the Thrissur Pooram festival, the most colourful and spectacular temple festival in Kerala. Located in the heart of Kerala, Thrissur can rightly be called the center of the state’s most distinguished beauty, keeper of history, protector of culture, and preserver of their long-running tradition. Visiting Thrissur will give you never before experience of exploring a city that has a lot to offer to its tourists from hill stations to waterfalls to historical monuments, and temples.

Check out the spots

Athirappilly Waterfalls

It is one of the best waterfalls not only in Thrissur but in the entire country. Many streams flow parallel to each other and form the waterfall. It is best if you visit this place during the monsoons. The waterfalls rests right at the entrance of the sholayar ranges.Athirappilly is a bird watchers paradise. Researchers have found four endangered species of the Hornbill here.

Kerala Kalamandalam

If you are a real art lover then this place can be the perfect destination for you. This place is a center of Indian culture and history and. It has now taken a form of campus and the whole area is well maintained. It may take a long time to roam all around the campus to view everything that this place houses. Some veteran teachers teach different forms of arts that prevail in the entirety of South India.

Shakthan Thampuran Palace

Of all the places to visit in Thrissur, the Shakthan Thampuran Palace is the most intriguing one. Once the seat of power of Shakthan Thampuran – the 18th-century ruler of the Kingdom of Cochin, this palace is built in the unusual Kerala Naalukettu and Dutch architectural styles. It was converted into a museum in the year 2005 and is visited for its beautiful architecture and its expansive collections on display.

Punnathur Kotta

Not so far away from the Guruvayoor Temple lies one of the best Thrissur tourist places, an Elephant Sanctuary, the Punnathur Kotta was initially a palace belonging to a local ruler. It was then converted to a house of elephants and now has the highest number captive elephant. This is where these beasts are trained to participate in several festivals. Apart from this, the Punnathur Kotta also has a training centre for Elephant Riders (Mahouts).
